Frequently Asked Questions

What is waste management? ❯

Waste management refers to the activities and actions required to manage waste from its inception to its final disposal. This includes collection, transportation, treatment, and disposal of waste, along with monitoring and regulation.

Why is waste management important? ❯

Waste management is crucial for maintaining a clean environment, reducing pollution, conserving resources, and ensuring the health and well-being of individuals and communities.

What are the types of waste? ❯

Waste can be categorized into different types, including solid waste (household garbage), liquid waste (sewage, wastewater), hazardous waste (chemicals, medical waste), and recyclable waste (paper, plastic, glass).

How can I reduce household waste? ❯

We can reduce household waste by recycling, composting organic waste, reusing items, buying products with minimal packaging, and reducing consumption of disposable products.

What is recycling? ❯

Recycling is the process of converting waste materials into new materials and objects. It helps reduce the consumption of fresh raw materials, decrease energy usage, and reduce air and water pollution.
